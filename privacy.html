Privacy Policy

How DocKit protects your data and respects your privacy

Last Updated: January 19, 2026

Overview

DocKit is committed to protecting the privacy and security of healthcare professionals and their patient data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard information when you use our medical AI assistant platform.

Information We Collect

Account Information

  • Email address (for authentication)
  • Professional credentials (optional)
  • Practice information (optional)

Usage Data

  • Voice transcriptions (stored locally on your device)
  • Consultation notes and session metadata
  • Application logs for debugging and improvement

Integration Data

  • Gmail and Google Calendar access tokens (OAuth)
  • Email metadata when searching patient communications
  • Calendar appointment information

How We Use Your Information

DocKit uses your information solely to provide and improve the medical assistant functionality:

  • Transcribe and document patient consultations
  • Search relevant patient communications in your email
  • Display your appointment schedule
  • Generate AI-powered insights and documentation
  • Improve application performance and reliability

Data Storage and Security

Local Storage

All consultation transcripts and patient data are stored locally on your device. DocKit does not transmit patient information to external servers except as required for the AI processing functions you explicitly use.

Third-Party Services

DocKit integrates with third-party services under your control:

  • Google Services: We access Gmail and Calendar only through OAuth with your explicit permission
  • AI Services: Voice transcription and AI analysis use Anthropic's Claude API with data processing agreements

Security Measures

  • OAuth token encryption
  • Local file system encryption
  • No cloud storage of patient data
  • Secure API communications (HTTPS)

HIPAA Compliance

DocKit is designed to support HIPAA-compliant workflows. As the healthcare provider, you are responsible for:

  • Ensuring your device and network security
  • Following your organization's data handling policies
  • Obtaining necessary patient consents
  • Maintaining physical security of devices running DocKit

Data Retention

You control all data retention:

  • Consultation transcripts remain on your device until you delete them
  • OAuth tokens can be revoked at any time through the Settings
  • Uninstalling DocKit removes all locally stored data

Third-Party Access

DocKit does not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes. We only share data with:

  • AI service providers (Anthropic) for processing transcriptions and generating insights
  • Google services when you explicitly authorize Gmail/Calendar integration

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access all data stored by DocKit on your device
  • Delete any or all consultation records
  • Revoke third-party integrations at any time
  • Export your data in standard formats
  • Request information about our data practices

Children's Privacy

DocKit is intended for use by licensed healthcare professionals only. We do not knowingly collect information from individuals under 18 years of age.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or for legal compliance. Material changes will be communicated through the application or via email.

Contact Us

For questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact:
